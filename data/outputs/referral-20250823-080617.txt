=== Clinical Summary ===
David Ignacio is an 8-year-old boy from Baja Verapaz, Guatemala who presented with a two-day history of fever, abdominal pain, nausea, vomiting and more than five episodes of liquid diarrhea per day. On examination he was listless, mildly dehydrated, febrile at 38.9 °C and tachycardic with a heart rate of 112 beats/min; his abdomen was soft with mild tenderness. The working diagnosis is acute gastroenteritis. He has been started on oral rehydration therapy to address fluid losses and prevent progression of dehydration. In addition, he is receiving amoxicillin 250 mg every six hours for seven days. He will require close monitoring of his hydration status, intake/output, and vital signs. Education has been provided on recognizing signs of worsening dehydration and on proper preparation and administration of oral rehydration solution.

=== Key Risks ===
- Progression to moderate or severe dehydration
- Electrolyte imbalances (e.g., hyponatremia)
- Adverse effects of antibiotics (e.g., diarrhea, allergic reaction)
- Development of antibiotic resistance
- Nutritional deficits due to ongoing gastrointestinal losses
- Potential for secondary bacterial or viral infections
